What I do

A quick overview of my day job, what I practice in labs, and why this site exists.

Day to day, I work in IT and security. That covers endpoint management, user access, onboarding, tickets, and supporting the systems people rely on without thinking about them.

Outside of work, I use lab environments to practice the parts of security that don’t always come up on a normal workday: enumeration, exploitation, and privilege escalation. It’s a good way to learn without wrecking anything important or stressing out coworkers.

This site is where I park notes, small writeups, and projects so they don’t just vanish into random folders. It’s not meant to be perfect, just honest and useful.

Labs & projects

A mix of lab work and real-world projects that shaped how I approach security and operations.

Lab projects

  • Privilege Escalation Practice
    Personal · Windows & Linux labs

    Ongoing work in lab environments focused on finding and exploiting common privilege escalation paths. I keep track of patterns that repeat across boxes instead of treating each one like a totally new puzzle.

  • First 30 Minutes Playbook
    Personal · Methodology over guessing

    A small checklist for new targets: network discovery, service enumeration, quick web checks, default creds, and obvious misconfigurations. The goal is to make the starting steps consistent instead of random.

  • Signal vs Noise Experiments
    Personal · Logs & alerting

    Tests around what actually gets logged, how alerts are triggered, and how tuning decisions change the volume and quality of security signals from endpoints.

Work projects

  • Endpoint Security Tuning in Production
    Investview Inc. · CrowdStrike Falcon

    Iterative tuning of endpoint security policies and host grouping so alerts are more meaningful. Worked on separating obvious noise from events that actually deserve time and follow-up.

  • Mail & Identity Migration
    Investview Inc. · Microsoft 365 → Google Workspace

    Helped move mail and identity: DNS changes, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, routing, archiving, and user cutover. Focused on keeping things working for users while cleaning up underlying configuration.

  • Access Review & Documentation Support
    ESD Inc. · PCI DSS 4.0 Readiness

    Assisted with access reviews, evidence collection, and written justifications. Treated it as a project in making risk and access decisions easier to explain later, not just ticking boxes.
